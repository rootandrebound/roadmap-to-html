Can I apply for CalWORKs while incarcerated?

No. You can only apply for CalWORKs once you have been released from prison or jail, and you have active custody of a child as a parent or caretaker.[1460] But if you‚Äôre currently incarcerated, you can and should look at the eligibility requirements so you are more aware of your options upon release.

How do I receive my CalWORKs benefits?

If you are eligible for CalWORKs benefits, you will be issued your benefits on an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card unless you request that your benefits are directly deposited into your personal bank account. You will need to set up a PIN number for the card to use it, and can then use the card at most grocery stores and ATMs.[1461]

To avoid unnecessary fees and surcharges when withdrawing your CalWORKs cash, you should only use the card at ATMs with the ‚ÄúMoneyPass‚Äù symbol, or the same ‚ÄúQuest Mark‚Äù symbol that appears on the card-sleeve.
