COMMON EXAMPLES OF CUSTODY ARRANGEMENTS

STORY #1: Jessica is an 8-year-old girl. The judge gave her mother, Maria, sole physical custody, meaning that Jessica lives with Maria full time. Jessica‚Äôs father, John, recently returned home from prison and asked the court for visitation. The court gave him weekly visitation with Jessica, but not any physical custody. However, the court gave both Maria and John joint legal custody over Jessica, which means they both get to make important decisions for her‚Äîlike medical decisions and where to go to school, etc. But only Maria has physical custody, meaning that only Maria is responsible for Jessica‚Äôs day-to-day care. To change this arrangement, John would need to go to court to ask for joint physical custody.

STORY #2: David‚Äôs parents, Carlos and Rashida, are separated, but the judge gave them joint physical custody and joint legal custody of their son. David lives with Rashida during most of the week (Monday-Thursday), and with Carlos on weekends (Friday-Sunday). Rashida and Carlos live in the same school district, so David can attend school normally during the week. Rashida and Carlos also share joint legal custody, which means they both get to make important decisions for their child‚Äîlike medical decisions, where to go to school, etc.
