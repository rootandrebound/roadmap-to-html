What was California‚Äôs Traffic Amnesty Program?

UPDATE: The Traffic Amnesty Program was a one-time opportunity to reduce overall fines on old traffic tickets that are eligible for the program.[2282] The program ended on March 31, 2017. The program helped 200,000 Californians get their driver license back after they had been suspended due to failure to appear in court (called ‚ÄúFTA‚Äù),[2283] OR for failure to pay traffic tickets/traffic fines (called ‚ÄúFTP‚Äù).[2284] Now that the program has ended, check with your local traffic court about any programs that help to reduce traffic court debt and reinstate licenses.

Also, if you have a Failure to Appear (FTA), Failure to Pay (FTP), or other failure to comply on your DMV record (which may be the reason your Driver License was suspended and/or make it difficult to renew your license), and the charge is more than 5 years old, you may be able to get the charge erased from your DMV record and reinstate your license by calling the DMV Mandatory Actions Line at 916-657-6525.[2285]
