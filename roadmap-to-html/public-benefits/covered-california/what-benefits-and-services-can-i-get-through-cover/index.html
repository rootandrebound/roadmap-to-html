What benefits and services can I get through Covered California?

As required by law, all Covered California plans must cover a set of essential health benefits, including:

    Hospital care and emergency services;
    Medical services;
    Prescription drugs;
    Mental health and substance abuse treatment (potentially including treatment that is a condition of probation or parole);
    Maternity and pediatric care;
    Rehabilitation services;
    Preventative care and chronic disease management; and
    Some dental care.[1508]

Can my criminal history limit my ability to get health care using Covered California?

SHARED RESPONSIBILITY FEES

For 2015, the “shared responsibility fee” is the higher of one of the following two amounts:

    2% of your yearly household income or $325 per person for the year.

No. There is no law or policy that limits your access to Covered California based on your criminal history. You have the right to apply for health care coverage, and you should.
